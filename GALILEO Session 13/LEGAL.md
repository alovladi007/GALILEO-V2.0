## Legal Research Restrictions

### PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES - Legal Violations

#### 1. Illegal Content and Activities
**NEVER engage in:**
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) - 18 U.S.C. § 2251, § 2252
- Human trafficking facilitation - 18 U.S.C. § 1591
- Drug trafficking or illegal substance distribution
- Money laundering facilitation - 18 U.S.C. § 1956
- Fraud or financial crimes
- Identity theft or personal information theft

#### 2. Weapons and National Security
**Strictly prohibited:**
- Development or distribution of weapons
- Violations of export control laws (ITAR, EAR)
- Support for designated terrorist organizations
- Classified information handling without authorization
- Circumvention of sanctions programs (OFAC)

#### 3. Cybercrime
**Illegal activities:**
- Unauthorized computer access - Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
- Distribution of malware or hacking tools
- DDoS attacks or network disruption
- Theft of trade secrets - Economic Espionage Act
- Circumvention of security measures - DMCA Section 1201

#### 4. Intellectual Property Violations
**Not permitted:**
- Copyright infringement - 17 U.S.C. § 501
- Patent infringement - 35 U.S.C. § 271
- Trademark infringement - 15 U.S.C. § 1114
- Trade secret misappropriation - Defend Trade Secrets Act
- Piracy or unauthorized distribution

## Data Protection Compliance

### GDPR Compliance Requirements

#### Lawful Basis for Processing
Data processing must have at least one lawful basis:
1. **Consent** - Clear, informed, freely given
2. **Contract** - Necessary for contract performance
3. **Legal Obligation** - Required by law
4. **Vital Interests** - Protect life of data subject
5. **Public Task** - Perform task in public interest
6. **Legitimate Interests** - Balanced against data subject rights

#### Data Subject Rights
Must facilitate:
- **Right to Access** - Provide copy of personal data
- **Right to Rectification** - Correct inaccurate data
- **Right to Erasure** - "Right to be forgotten"
- **Right to Restriction** - Limit processing
- **Right to Data Portability** - Transfer data
- **Right to Object** - Object to processing
- **Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making**

#### Privacy by Design
- Data protection from system design phase
- Default privacy settings
- Data minimization principles
- Pseudonymization and encryption
- Regular privacy impact assessments

#### International Data Transfers
- Adequacy decisions (EU Commission approved countries)
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
- Derogations for specific situations only
- Transfer Impact Assessments required

### CCPA/CPRA Compliance

#### Consumer Rights
- **Right to Know** - What data is collected
- **Right to Delete** - Request deletion of data
- **Right to Opt-Out** - Sale/sharing of personal information
- **Right to Correct** - Inaccurate personal information
- **Right to Limit** - Use of sensitive personal information
- **Non-Discrimination** - For exercising rights

#### Business Obligations
- Privacy notice requirements
- Response to consumer requests (45 days)
- Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information link
- Opt-in consent for minors under 16
- Data security measures

## Security and Breach Requirements

### Data Security Measures
- Encryption in transit and at rest
- Access controls and authentication
- Security monitoring and logging
- Vulnerability management
- Incident response procedures

### Breach Notification
**GDPR Requirements:**
- Notify supervisory authority within 72 hours
- Notify affected individuals without undue delay
- Document all breaches

**CCPA Requirements:**
- Notify California Attorney General if 500+ residents affected
- Provide required information elements

**Other Jurisdictions:**
- Comply with state breach notification laws
- Industry-specific notification requirements (HIPAA, etc.)

## Legal Hold and Litigation

### Legal Hold Procedures
1. **Trigger Events**
   - Receipt of litigation notice
   - Government investigation
   - Regulatory inquiry
   - Internal investigation

2. **Implementation**
   - Identify custodians
   - Preserve relevant data
   - Suspend routine deletion
   - Document hold notices
   - Monitor compliance

3. **Release**
   - Legal approval required
   - Document release decision
   - Resume normal retention
   - Maintain hold records

### E-Discovery Requirements
- Maintain data in accessible formats
- Implement search and retrieval capabilities
- Produce data in requested formats
- Privilege logs for withheld documents
- Cost allocation considerations

## Incident Response and Investigation

### Legal Incident Types
- Data breaches
- Privacy violations
- IP infringement claims
- Regulatory violations
- Contractual breaches

### Response Procedures
1. **Immediate Actions** (0-24 hours)
   - Contain incident
   - Preserve evidence
   - Notify legal counsel
   - Begin investigation

2. **Assessment** (24-72 hours)
   - Scope of incident
   - Legal obligations triggered
   - Affected parties identification
   - Risk assessment

3. **Notification** (As required)
   - Regulatory authorities
   - Affected individuals
   - Business partners
   - Law enforcement (if criminal)

4. **Remediation**
   - Implement corrective measures
   - Update policies and procedures
   - Training and awareness
   - Monitor effectiveness

## Penalties and Enforcement

### GDPR Penalties
- Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever higher)
- Specific violations: Up to €10 million or 2% of turnover

### CCPA/CPRA Penalties
- Civil penalties: Up to $2,500 per violation
- Intentional violations: Up to $7,500 per violation
- Private right of action for data breaches

### Other Consequences
- Criminal prosecution (for serious violations)
- Injunctions and cease and desist orders
- Contractual damages
- Reputational harm
- Loss of business licenses
